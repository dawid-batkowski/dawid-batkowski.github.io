P2 - Frogment

Frogment - Project Pitch

"In this magical puzzle solving game, you play as the Wizard Frog. Their goal is to find the Firefly Queen. Stay alert! The Mysterious Valley is full of tricky obstacles, pressure plates, rotating bridges, maze-like stair complexes and cumbersome ladders. Are you clever enough to find your way to the end?"

Frogment - Overview

Specifications:
- Engine: Unity
- Platform: Mobile
- My Role: Environmental Art, Technical Art

The game features four distinct levels, each with its own unique environment and puzzle design.
The interactive environment allows the player to drag and rotate platform structures to create a path for the frog to traverse.

My role in the project focused on creating shaders and particle systems to enhance the visual quality of the environments. In addition, I supported the art pipeline by producing environmental 3D assets such as trees, mountains, crystals, and rocks.

Featured Work - Water Shader

My primary contribution to this project was the development of a water shader. The goal was to create a flexible and reusable solution that could be adapted to produce distinct water styles across different levels. To achieve this, the shader exposes a wide range of parameters, allowing artists and designers to easily customize the final look to fit each environment.

The shader is applied to a plane, either covering large areas of a level or used locally for smaller water bodies. It utilizes Unity’s procedural noise functions to simulate effects such as water foam, floating cherry blossom petals, depth-based coloration, and wave motion.
